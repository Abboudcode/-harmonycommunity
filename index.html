What kind of stories belong here?

Harmony is not for perfect families. It’s for real life – the parts we usually hide.

  • • Parents who feel guilty for shouting, but don’t know what else to do.
  • • Teens who feel nobody listens and are scared to speak at home.
  • • Couples who love each other but are stuck in the same fights.
  • • Adults who feel empty while trying to make everyone else happy.
  • • Anyone stressed about money, debt, or providing for the family.

If it stays on your mind at night, it belongs here. Even if you think “my problem is not big enough” – your feelings still matter.

Why Harmony exists

Harmony is being built by parents and families who know how it feels to smile outside and break inside.

We created Harmony because so many people carry family pain in silence — afraid of gossip, judgment, or “advice” that actually hurts. Social media is loud, fast and often cruel. Harmony is the opposite: slow, soft, honest. A place where you can say “I’m not okay” and be met with understanding instead of blame.

Our Promise to You

  • ● We protect your privacy and give you control over what you share.
  • ● We design for empathy, not drama or arguments.
  • ● We keep Harmony free from bullying and disrespect.
  • ● We remember that behind every post is a real human heart.

Questions people usually ask

Here are quick answers so you know exactly what Harmony is – and what it isn’t.

Is Harmony completely anonymous?

In the full app you’ll be able to choose: post with your real name, a nickname, or as “Anonymous”. Your feelings stay real either way.

Is Harmony a replacement for therapy?

No. Harmony is a community space for support and understanding, not medical or professional advice. It can walk with you, not replace a doctor or therapist.

Will people judge me if my story sounds small?

Here, we believe if it keeps you awake at night, it is big enough. Harmony is designed to reduce shame, not increase it.

When will the real app be ready?

This site is an early preview. I’m building Harmony step by step. If you like the idea, your feedback and email will help decide the next features.
